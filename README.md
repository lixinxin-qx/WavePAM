Multivariate time series anomaly detection is critical for ensuring the reliability of industrial systems. However, existing multi-scale methods inherently suffer from oversmoothing, which often obliterates short-duration, transient anomalies. Concurrently, conventional frequency-domain approaches and memory networks rely on uninformative priors, such as global Fourier spectra or Gaussian initializations, struggling to construct a highly discriminative latent space. To address these critical bottlenecks, we propose WavePAM (Wavelet-driven Perception and Asymmetric Memory), a novel framework that moves beyond oversmoothing via dynamics-aware asymmetric refinement and orthogonal wavelet memory. Specifically, an asymmetric time-frequency paradigm based on wavelet decomposition is introduced to leverage macroscopic low-frequency trends for semantically filtering noisy high-frequency details. To explicitly counteract the oversmoothing issue, a dynamics-aware multi-scale encoder incorporates temporal gradient priors, successfully preserving fleeting anomalies. Furthermore, breaking away from traditional uninformative memory, a structured, orthogonally constrained wavelet prototype space is constructed alongside hierarchical frequency-domain attention. This architecture optimally encodes normal operational patterns and significantly amplifies the representation gap between normal and anomalous states. Extensive experiments on five real-world benchmarks (SMD, PSM, SWAT, MSL, SMAP) demonstrate that WavePAM consistently outperforms ten state-of-the-art baselines in F1-score.
